Bugatti Bolide History

Bugatti is storied and historic company that has been through a number of different hands and produced many iconic vehicles. Here is a brief overview of the events leading up to the Bugatti Bolide:

  • 1909 - A certain Mr. Ettore Bugatti founds a company in Germany.
  • 1934 - Bugatti begins production of the iconic Type 57 "Atlantic" luxury coupe.
  • 1939 - Bugatti wins the famous 24 Hours of Le Mans for the second time in one of their own cars.
  • 1940 - If cars are going well, why not try airplanes? This year marked the shelving of the beautiful but flightless 100p race plane.
  • 1947 - Ettore Bugatti passes away, dealing a large blow to the company and leaving no successors.
  • 1963 - After years of decline and financial struggle, Bugatti is sold off as a parts company.
  • 1987 - Italian entrepreneur Romano Artioli revives the company as Bugatti S.p.A.
  • 1991 - Bugatti produces its first car in decades, the iconic EB110.
  • 1995 - Economic downturns lead to the second failure of the company. Bugatti's assets are liquidated and the factory sold to a furniture company.
  • 1998 - Volkswagen Group comes to the rescue, bringing the company back from the dead as Bugatti S.A.S.
  • 2005 - The groundbreaking Bugatti Veyron is officially put into production.
  • 2019 - The Chiron, successor to the Veyron, becomes one of the first production cars to exceed 300 mph.
  • 2020 - Bugatti officially unveils the Bolide.
  • 2024 - (Projected) Bugatti delivers the first of the 40 Bolides that will be manufactured.
